Transition Year Work Experince

During my transition year in secondary school, we had to organise 2 different weeks work experince. My first week I returned to my primary school. I was helping out with first class, and senior infants. I was assiting the teacher with things like organising an photocopying etc, as well as helping the students with their class work and reading. Although i enjoyed the week, it helped me realise that I was not interested in primary school teaching. My second week was spent in a law firm called Ronan Daly Jermyn(RDJ). It was a much more professional environment in comparison to the primary school. During the week, each person doing their work experince was partnered with an intern. We got to help read all of their cases and learn about all the different areas of expertise in law. At the start of the week, we were also assigned a case and the peple split doing the work experince were split into teams. At the end of the week, we were lucky enough to complete a mock trial where the two teams went head to head and presented our cases along with everything we learned throughout the week. On the second last day, we were brought to the courthouse to sit in on a trial too which was incredibly interesting. The whole week was very enjoyable and very insightful.
